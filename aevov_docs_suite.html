The Resonon |𝕄, χ, φ⟩ is the base unit of Resonance Physics computation. Every qubit is a Resonon at χ=2, 𝕄→1.

python# rpu_primitives.py class Resonon: def __init__(self, M=0.0, chi=1, phi=0.0): self.M = float(max(0.0, min(1.0, M))) self.chi = max(1, int(chi)) self.phi = phi % (2 * math.pi) def is_qubit(self) -> bool: return self.chi == 2 and self.M > 0.99 def impedance(self) -> float: return (1 - self.M) / self.chi # Z_M

FAQ Frequently Asked Questions
Q: Does RP invalidate quantum mechanics?

No. QM is preserved as the 𝕄→1, χ=2 limit of RP. Every QPU algorithm runs unchanged on an RPU at those parameter values. RP extends the design space; it does not replace the existing one.

Q: How is RP different from Quantum Resonance Theory (QRT) or QRD?

QRT modifies the Schrödinger equation with a resonance coupling term within standard qubit formalism — no 𝕄, no coherence spectrum, no biological coupling, no DOI. QRD proposes chirality as the mechanism for structured collapse — structurally unrelated to 𝕄 and Z_M, no particle mass derivations, no DOI, no platform. RP is formally distinct from both on every foundational axis.

Q: Is the RPU a quantum computer?

RPU stands for Resonon Processing Unit — the processing unit that operates on Resonon registers |𝕄, χ, φ⟩. It is a third computational paradigm distinct from both classical CPUs (Binary Logic) and quantum QPUs (Qubit Logic). It operates in the resonant regime (0 < 𝕄 < 1), supports qubit-equivalent operation at 𝕄→1, χ=2, and extends the design space beyond qubits with d>2 bond dimensions. It is not a QPU.

Q: Is the prior art legally binding?
